Project Overview

This stunning 3,500 sq ft residence in Beverly Hills showcases our expertise in creating serene, functional spaces through the principles of minimalist design. The project was a complete interior transformation of a mid-century modern home that had lost its original character through previous renovations.

Our client, a tech entrepreneur, sought a calming retreat from her busy professional life. She wanted a home that embodied simplicity, featured clean lines, and utilized natural materials while maintaining the warmth and comfort essential for daily living.

We approached this project with a focus on creating a cohesive flow throughout the home, emphasizing natural light, and carefully curating a neutral color palette accented with warm wood tones. Every element was thoughtfully selected to serve both aesthetic and functional purposes, embodying the "less is more" philosophy.

The result is a harmonious living environment that balances minimalism with livability, proving that simplicity doesn't mean sacrificing comfort or personality.

The Challenge

The existing space had been heavily compartmentalized with dated renovations that blocked natural light and created a disjointed flow. Dark, heavy finishes throughout made the home feel smaller than its actual square footage. The client also needed better storage solutions without compromising the minimalist aesthetic.

Our Solution

We removed non-structural walls to create an open-concept living area, installed floor-to-ceiling windows to maximize natural light, and implemented custom built-in storage throughout. A neutral color palette with warm wood accents created visual continuity, while carefully selected furniture pieces maintained the minimalist ethos without sacrificing functionality.
